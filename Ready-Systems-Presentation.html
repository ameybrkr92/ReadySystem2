Inside the engine

Config → harness BOM → cost & quote

The owner’s pricing logic, captured once and applied consistently — margin is the only dial you turn per quote.

MaterialLocked BOM × master rates
+ Wastage% for offcuts & rejects
+ LabourBuild-minutes × rate/min
+ Overhead% for power, floor, supervision
= Works costSubtotal before margin
+ MarginTuned per quote
= Quote totalTotal & per-unit

What we need to firm it up

A 30–45 min walkthrough: for each feeder type, the wires, lengths & build time — plus one filled costing sheet and 3–5 past orders with their quoted price. Today’s library & labour times are sensible placeholders until then.

Talking point: Be honest that numbers are placeholders — the walkthrough is the single biggest input we need from them.

How work flows

One work order, eleven stages, three gates

The job number (your Siemens W/O) ties everything together — quote approval opens buying, and material can’t move without an inspection record.

01 RFQ02 Final BOM03 Costing04 Quote05 Approved ✓06 PO 07 Incoming QC ⛬08 Stores09 Build10 Final QC ⛬11 Dispatch

Gate 1 — Approval

Procurement only opens once the client approves the quote.

Gate 2 — Incoming QC

Goods are inspected before they enter stock.

Gate 3 — Final QC

Nothing ships without passing the pre-dispatch check.

Talking point: The gates are guardrails — they prevent the “bought against a job that changed” and “shipped without a record” mistakes.

Inside a job

The order workspace — a guided, gated pipeline

Open any order and walk it from drawings to dispatch; each step unlocks the next.

1 · Documents

Schematics, layout boards, GA drawings travel with the job.

2 · BOM

Engine-generated harness; lock it to freeze and open costing.

3 · Costing & Quote

Full cost build-up, set margin, send the quote.

4 · Approval

Record the client’s decision — the gate to buying.

5 · Procurement

Order the shortfall; receipts update from Stores.

6 · Build & recovery

Floor progress, final QC, dispatch — and rework if it fails.

Talking point: A “Next” banner always points to the single most useful action, so nothing falls through the cracks.

Recently improved

Quoting is a proper client handshake New

A quote can no longer be “self-approved” — approval is deliberate and evidenced.

1 · Send

Quote goes out; order shows Sent — awaiting approval. Buying stays locked.

2 · Record decision

Capture the client’s approval reference (their PO / email) and date — or a rejection reason.

3 · Unlock

Only a recorded approval moves the job to Approved and opens procurement.

Talking point: This gives them a clean audit trail of who approved what, and when — useful for disputes and for the Director view.

12
Stores & Quality

Receipts that update themselves; QC with a rework loop New

Inventory receives & inspects, issues to the job; Quality gates dispatch and tracks the metrics that matter.

Stores

  • Goods inward against a PO → auto-updates the PO
  • Incoming QC: Accept / Rework / Reject (audit record)
  • Live stock; issue to a job sends it to Build

Quality

  • Floor marks build complete to reach Final QC
  • Pass · Hold · Reject → rework (recovery loop)
  • KPIs: first-pass yield, on-time delivery, supplier rejection, COPQ, complaints, audit obs
Talking point: Rejected jobs go back to the floor with their failed report attached — the operator sees exactly what to fix.
