01 — Overview

What it is & why now

Ready Systems builds harnesses and MV switchgear (8DJHST / 8FB20) on a contract-assembly basis. Everything runs on registers and Excel today.

The four costs of registers & Excel

It's slow (quoting depends on one person's memory), fragile (one file, lost rows, no backup), invisible (no live view of stock or job status), and hard to audit (paper quality records). None of these are about how they build — they're experts at that. They're about the paperwork and coordination around the build.

Where software wins

We don't change how they work — we make it digital, searchable, backed-up and connected. One record per work order, moving through known stages, with the owner's costing know-how captured so quoting stops depending on memory. We already understand their world: inward register, controlled quality plans, Siemens order tracker, and the decoded RMU configuration codes.

The principle that guides everything

Prototype-first, mirror their reality, honour their expertise, free pilot with a graceful exit — and Tally stays for accounting; we connect to it, we don't rebuild it.

02 — Vision

The two-phase vision

Own the pre-digital factory first, then layer intelligence on top. Each step delivers value on its own.

Phase 1

Bring everything online — the foundation

Every register and Excel sheet becomes a connected digital system. Planning/BOM (the order master replaces the Siemens-orders Excel), Purchase (POs triggered from the BOM), Stores (goods inward + running stock + issue-to-job), Quality (controlled plans + audit-ready records) — all connected by one job ID, with Tally kept for accounting.

Phase 2

The intelligence layer — the leap

Stop typing what a machine can read. QR / invoice scan auto-fills Stores from a supplier challan; the same auto-capture across purchase receipts, BOM imports and QC; analytics on quoting turnaround, stock trends, supplier quality and estimate-vs-actual cost; and a smart costing engine that learns from past orders.

Why this order is right

Intelligence needs data to be intelligent about. You can't scan-to-fill a system that doesn't exist yet. Phase 1 creates the digital foundation; Phase 2 makes it smart — and they get a working system after Phase 1, not a half-thing waiting on Phase 2.

03 — The POC

First, prove the costing engine

Before committing to the full build, we prove the riskiest, highest-value thing — not a thin slice of everything.

The crown jewel is the costing engine: turn a Siemens order (e.g. 8DJHST RRL+ME, motorised) into a harness BOM and a cost, fast, without depending entirely on the owner's memory. That's the hardest part, the highest-value part, and the thing that makes them say "we can't go back." This prototype already demonstrates it — type a config, the engine parses the feeders, assembles the harness from a feeder library, prices it from the material master, and produces a cost and quote.

POC scope (small & sharp)

  • Order master — enter/import a Siemens order
  • Feeder library — decoded configs (R/L/T/K + ME), each linked to a harness set
  • Costing engine — parse config → assemble harness BOM → apply rates → cost + quote
  • Run on 3–5 real past orders; check cost vs what they actually quoted

What the POC leaves out (for now)

  • Full Purchase / Stores / Quality depth (Phase 1)
  • QR / invoice scanning (Phase 2)
  • Tally integration, multi-user, production polish

This build already sketches all four modules so the partner can see the whole shape — but the POC's job is to prove the engine.

What we need to firm up the engine

A 30–45 min walkthrough with the owner — for each feeder type (R, L, T, K), the wires + lengths + build time — plus one real filled costing sheet and 3–5 real past orders with their quoted price. The feeder library and labour times in this prototype are sensible placeholders until then.

04 — Lifecycle

The order lifecycle

One work order, one job ID (the Siemens W/O No), moving through eleven stages with built-in gates. Quote approval opens procurement, and two hard quality gates mean material cannot pass without an inspection record.

01 RFQ02 Final BOM03 Costing04 Quote05 Approved ✓06 PO 07 Incoming QC ⛬08 Stores09 Build10 Final QC ⛬11 Dispatch

Stages advance as work is done: finalise & lock the BOM → Costing; send a quote → Quote; approve the quote → Approved (this opens procurement); raise/award a PO → PO; log goods inward in Stores → Incoming QC (receipt is automatic — no separatee "mark received"); accept material → Stores; issue to the job → Build; pass final QC → Dispatch. Any order can be flagged stuck with a reason, which surfaces instantly on the Director board.

One job ID connects all four modules

Planning, Purchase, Stores and Quality all hang off the same W/O. That's what makes the Director's single live view — and the per-job procurement status — possible.
