01 — Why now

Everything runs on registers and Excel

The four costs of paper & spreadsheets

It's slow — quoting depends on one person's memory. Fragile — one file, lost rows, no backup. Invisible — no live view of stock or job status. And hard to audit — paper quality records.

Where software wins

We don't change how they build — they're experts at that. We make the paperwork digital, searchable, backed-up and connected: one record per work order, moving through known stages, with the owner's costing know-how captured.

The principle that guides everything

Prototype-first, mirror their reality, honour their expertise, free pilot with a graceful exit — and Tally stays for accounting; we connect to it, we don't rebuild it.

02 — The vision

Own the pre-digital factory first.
Then layer intelligence on top.

Two phases, each delivering value on its own. Phase 1 brings every register and Excel sheet online, connected by one job ID. Phase 2 adds the leap — scan-to-fill, analytics and a costing engine that learns. Intelligence needs data to be intelligent about.

03 — The crown jewel

First, prove the costing engine

Turn a Siemens order — 8DJHST RRL+ME, motorised — into a harness BOM and a cost, fast, without depending entirely on the owner's memory.

That's the hardest part, the highest-value part, and the thing that makes them say "we can't go back." This prototype already demonstrates it, end to end.

POC scope — small & sharp

  • Order master — enter or import a Siemens order
  • Feeder library — decoded configs (R/L/T/K + ME), each linked to a harness set
  • Costing engine — parse config → assemble BOM → apply rates → cost + quote
  • Validate on 3–5 real past orders vs what they actually quoted

One job ID connects all four modules

Quote approval opens procurement. Two hard quality gates mean material can't pass without an inspection record. Planning, Purchase, Stores and Quality all hang off the same W/O — that's what makes the Director's single live view possible.

05 — How it works

A guided, gated pipeline — not a pile of tabs

Open any W/O and a stepper shows exactly where it stands and the single next action. Each step unlocks the next and locks the last — so the process is mistake-proof by design.

1 · DocumentsSchematics, layout boards (3D view), GA drawings — attached before the BOM
2 · BOMEngine builds the harness. Edit, then Finalize & Lock. Changing it needs a Revise → Rev n+1
3 · CostingLocked until the BOM is locked. Live margin slider, per-unit price, send quote
4 · ApprovalThe gate. After approval, costing locks so the price can't drift
5 · ProcurementDoesn't exist until approval. Order only the shortfall; receipt is automatic

10 — Honest review

Where it stands

As a POC it does its job: the costing engine works end-to-end on realistic data, the four modules are connected by one job ID, and planning now has a forward-looking spine. The model of the business is right — it speaks the shop's language.

It is, deliberately, not production software yet: state lives in the browser, logins are demo-only, no shared database — exactly the right trade for a free, no-infrastructure pilot.

The one input that unlocks the rest

The feeder library — wires, lengths, build-times — is a placeholder. The owner walkthrough is the first real input we need; then we validate against 3–5 past quotes and we have our proof.
